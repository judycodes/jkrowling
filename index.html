Chapter One

Life Story

J. K. Rowling as many readers know her as was born Joanne Rowling on July 31, 1965 in Yate, Gloucestershire, England. Her childhood was complicated by a mother with multiple sclerosis and a poor relationship with her father. Throughout it, she was a voracious reader.

J. K. Rowling conceived the idea of the Harry Potter series on a delayed train in 1990 and worked on bringing the story to life over the course of five years. Her first book was first published by Bloomsbury Children’s Books in June 1997 to much record-breaking success. Movies, more books, and even a play followed, making J. K. Rowling a household name.

Contributions and Causes

Besides the lives she has affected through her stories and advice, she contributes and supports many causes. Her charitable trust, Volant focuses on women and children affected by hardship, while her international children's charity, Lumos aims to end institutionalisation around the world by 2050. J. K. Rowling also supports Multiple Sclerosis research through the Anne Rowling Regenerative Neurology Clinic. Her experiences has clearly directed her philanthropic endeavors.
